# Nextdoor post for Texas Water Watchers

**💧 You can now see exactly how much groundwater is under our part of Kendall County. I built a free live tracker for our whole area.**

Neighbors, most of us are on wells out here, but it's hard to know what the aquifer under us is actually doing. So I pulled the official readings from the **Cow Creek Groundwater Conservation District's monitoring network** (hosted by the Texas Water Development Board) and put all of it on one simple map anyone can check:

👉 **https://www.texaswaterwatchers.com**

**Find the well nearest you.** There are **23 monitoring wells across the county**, from Comfort and Waring to Sisterdale, Kendalia, and all around Boerne. Tap any dot on the map (or use the menu) to see that well's water level, history, and how deep it's drilled.

**How to read it:** the number is *how far down you'd have to go to reach water.* So a **bigger number is bad**. It means the water has dropped deeper underground and there's less of it left.

Here's what the data shows, and it's sobering:

💧 Near Boerne, one well has fallen to **about 475 feet, its lowest level ever recorded, and roughly 140 feet deeper than it was in 2015.**

💧 The well near Comfort is **about 96 ft lower** than its high, and the Waring well (records back to **1997**) is **about 106 ft lower.**

💧 **4 of the 23 wells are sitting within a few feet of the lowest level ever recorded** at those sites.

There's also a diagram of each well, drawn to scale, showing how far the water has retreated down the borehole, and it updates on its own each day so it stays current.

We're all drawing from the same aquifer, and these are the wells that measure it. Worth a look, and worth passing along. 💧
